Description

Fried rice is one of Indonesia's most iconic dishes, known and loved by many, both domestically and internationally. This dish is simple yet full of flavor, consisting of rice stir-fried with spices and additional ingredients that can vary according to taste. However, behind its simplicity, fried rice has a rich history and diverse variations that reflect the culinary diversity of the archipelago.

The popularity of fried rice is not limited to Indonesia; it has also spread to various other countries. In some places, fried rice is known as "fried rice" and has been adapted to local flavors while still maintaining its original essence. Steps

  1. Assemble ingredients
  2. Place carrots in a small saucepan and cover with water. Bring to a low boil and cook for 3 to 5 minutes. Stir in peas, then immediately drain in a colander.
  3. Heat a wok over high heat. Pour in vegetable oil, then stir in carrots, peas, and garlic; cook for about 30 seconds. Add eggs; stir quickly to scramble eggs with vegetables.
  4. Stir in cooked rice. Add soy sauce and toss rice to coat. Drizzle with sesame oil and toss again.
  5. Serve hot and enjoy!
